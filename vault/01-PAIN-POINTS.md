# MedBill Hacker — Pain Points

## Primary Pain Point
> **"My hospital bill is $4,500 and I have no idea if it's accurate. I can't afford it."**

### 1. Illegal Upcoding
- **Problem:** Hospitals routinely assign higher-cost CPT codes than the service warranted (e.g., Level 5 ER visit for a sprained ankle).
- **Scale:** 80% of US medical bills contain billing errors (Medical Billing Advocates of America).
- **Average overcharge:** $1,300 per hospital visit.

### 2. Surprise Billing
- **Problem:** Patients go to an in-network hospital but get treated by an out-of-network doctor without consent.
- **Law:** The No Surprises Act (2022) makes this illegal, but hospitals still do it.

### 3. Duplicate Charges
- **Problem:** Hospitals charge separately for items already included in the room rate (recovery room, IV supplies).
- **Why It Persists:** Bills are intentionally opaque — 300+ line items nobody can parse.

### 4. Medicare Rate Gap
- **Problem:** Hospitals charge private patients 3-10x what Medicare pays for the identical procedure.
- **Our Solution:** AI cross-references the hospital's CPT codes against the CMS Medicare Fee Schedule by zip code.

## User Personas
- **"Overwhelmed Patient"** (Age 25-65): Just got a $5k ER bill, scared, doesn't know their rights.
- **"Uninsured Worker"** (Age 20-40): No insurance, facing full "chargemaster" prices (often 10x Medicare rate).
- **"Parent of Sick Child"** (Age 28-50): Pediatric bills are especially inflated; emotional vulnerability reduces negotiation.

## Current Alternatives
1. **Pay the full bill** (60%) — Most people just pay or set up a payment plan
2. **Call and beg** (25%) — Spend hours on hold asking for a discount
3. **Hire a medical billing advocate** (5%) — Costs $200-$500/hour
4. **File bankruptcy** (10% of medical debt holders) — Nuclear option
